> UNII Corps log date 2327-09-30-1004 2h later

Kane Carthatch idly watched the buildings rush away underneath them. Quiet and supposedly calming music played through
his headset in a halfhearted attempt to overcome the aggressive thrum of the helicopter blades. Underneath, buildings
whirred past at they sped towards the headquarters tower. Kane was perturbed at being called in personally, and even
more so at being directly requested by an underling, but he allowed the little people their moments. He had been told it
was important. So Kane idly watched the horizon, and did his best to enjoy the ride. He did not enjoy it, but sometimes
he forced himself to try these things. THe little people demanded it.

With a bump, Kane remained seated while the blades slowed to an acceptable level. He took one last sip of a fancy
coffee, and got up to disembark. Without an utterance, the door slid open on perfectly oiled hinges and someone held an
umbrella above the exit. He already knew who it was, and without breaking stride he stepped out and down the step onto
the rooftop pad. Their was a small breeze, but thankfully the original architect had had the foresight to build the pad
recessed into the roof to protect the occupants from the harsh winds. In his younger years, Kane had been curious to
learn about this diviner of architects, but his father had simply told him to ignore it and focus on the business needs.
_The little people will not matter._ Kane still thought on those words often. Nobody would matter when it was all said
and done, but some would last longer than others.

"Sir I've prepped your office. You're to begin with Dr Ziatovsicsh first. After that you have a follow-up with Mr
Kalospov concerning the acquisition of viatech." said the familiar voice of his assistant, Tailings. Kane didn't
actually know his first name.

"Hmmf." Kane grumbled, knowing full well that his first meeting was with the Doctor. Ziatovsicsh had been the one to
schedule this meeting on the insistence that it would be 'the most important meeting he'd have all month'. The double
doors at the end of th pad slid silently open and a rush of air swirled about them. He left Tailings, who was shaking
water from the umbrella and made for the meeting room. He unconsciously smoothed his hair, consciously aware that it was
starting to grey more and more. The constant attrition of running this corporation was taking it's toll on him.
Physically, he felt fine, but he couldn't help but worry that his appearance was slipping. Meticulous grooming was
always necessary for leadership. _Looking the part was just as important as being the part._

Kane rounded the corner and arrived at his office. The doors glided open, and he saw that against his preference
Ziatovsicsh was already in his office. _How fucking dare he._ Kane did not make eye contact, as he mentally made a note
to fire whomever had let this man into his office. Ziatovsicsh had the good graces to wait for him to walk past, and he
made the considerable distance to his desk. Kane had, of course, made it this way. He'd left the office completely
baren, and had put his desk at the far end. The effect had proved quite unnerving for some, and Kane had relished in
their discomfort. Having the reverse being done to him, he felt a flush of anger cross his mask, only disappearing with
a willful quelling on his part. More than once Kane had questioned if keeping this man employed here was a good idea,
and right now he was strongly against it. 'Irreplaceable' people just meant more expensive people.

Kane arrived, and could not help but drag his hand across his desk. An ancient habit that he did not bother to
relinquish just because of Ziatovsicsh. He felt the smooth and lacquered grain against his fingers. Real wood was a
rarity these days, but this desk had been in his family for generations. He, and all his forbearers, had probably ran
their fingers across it a million times, just like this, and would a million times more. His chair faced him, and he
turned to sit in its luxurious leather. Savouring the moment, while simultaneously dragging his emotions deep down into
himself. He sat for just a heartbeat.

Without a word he waved the doctor forwards. Ziatovsicsh stepped quickly, and Kane used the spare seconds he had bought
with his little flourish to size him up. Ziatovsicsh barely slept in the past, and only seemed to bother with
appearances as needed. This was a new low for him, now. He looked even more bleary than ever, his skin pale and sweaty.
His lab coat, pants, and his sweater all looked like he had been sleeping in them for multiple days. It was his only
saving grace, that Kane could not smell him. _Most important meeting my ass._ Ziatovsicsh came to a stop, adjusted his
glasses, and took a breath.

"Thank you fo-"

"Yes yes, if you're going to just barge in here, might as well ignore the pleasantries and get to it." Kane interrupted
him, doing his level best to keep any drip of anger from his voice.

"Alphason is alive." Ziatovsicsh blurted out.

"What the fu-", Kane could not help but sputter in response.

"But that's not the important part" Ziatovsicsh finished.

"Didn't I give him to you with the explicit understanding that you were going to kill him? Wasn't it you moaning about
how we shouldn't waste good experiments?!" Kane snapped at him.

"It's not important."

"The hell it is!" Kane stood up from his desk. He did his best to tower over the doctor, but they were about the same
height, so he had to make do with glaring at him.

"No it's not. What's important is what he survived through, and what it's done to him. Do you remember the satellite
monitoring room incident?"

Kane was honestly deciding whether to just have this man thrown from the roof, so he simply nodded.

"Well we investigated the cause, because it wasn't just a freak electrocution incident like we originally thought.
There's a signal. Those hapless technicians were recorded watching the raw output before they died." The doctor paused
clearly trying to get a read on Kane's face. "Well we did some investigation, and we discovered that it was the signal
itself that killed them, we still don't know how but exposure to it causes a fatal overload in the human brain by
unknown means."

Kane was getting tired of waiting for Ziatovsicsh to get to the point. He was clearly manic from one too many all
nighters and this was the result. "They were killed by watching a movie? A computer screen?" Kane said.

"Yes. No. Not just them. We've recreated it, and replicated the results. And even more so." Ziatovsicsh paused and
tilted his tablet towards Kane.

Kane did his best to lower his gaze from the doctor, and spent the next few seconds trying to parse what he was trying
to show him. Between his shaking hands, Kane could make out a spreadsheet with the first column a list of names.
Ziatovsicsh's hands were shaking too much for him to properly see it, and he reached for the tablet, motioning the
doctor to hand it to him. Ziatovsicsh paused for a second. He was normally fully cooperative, or even excited to have
someone take an interest. This reluctance spoke volumes. He handed him the tablet and he tilted it to properly read the
spreadsheet.

"You see we've been trying to isolate the variables, test how it works, and even how much exposure is required.
Obviously it's been challenging since we can only rely of third person testing. I've already lost a dozen lab
technicians and assistants to mistakes in the process."

Kane only half paid attention, scrolling through the list. Now that he was holding it, he could see it was much more
expansive that he had originally thought. It had multiple columns with headers like 'date', 'duration', 'outcome',
'procedure type', and 'special notes'. He noticed that the 'outcome' column had identical values all the way down.
Except for one at the bottom.

Kane did not need to read the name to put two and two together.

"How did he do it?" Kane asked dryly. "How did Alphason survive your little experiments?"

"He didn't, that's just it!" Ziatovsicsh said, almost out of breath.

Kane read the spreadsheet row a little more closely. He tapped on the 'special notes' cell and read the entry.

"Subject expired, then revived several days later. He then proceeded to destroy the processing facility and escape,
killing dozens in the process, <datestamp> ?" Kane read aloud, unable to keep a growing tone of incredulity from
creeping in with each word. "What kind of doctor are you?"

"Sir?" Ziatovsicsh sputtered.

"You fail to kill someone I expressly said to. You barge in here acting like this is good news. You've clearly been
siting on this all week and can't even bother to tell me when it happened." He said, indicating the date stamp on the
notes. "You work for me doctor. You answer to me. When something happens you report it to me. Not weeks later, now. When
I give you a task you had better do it. You're dismissed. I'm getting Knopp to clean this up."

"No wait, that's not the important part. It's what I'm trying to tell you." Ziatovsicsh gasped. "It's what happened
after he escaped that I'm trying to get to. Ever since Alphason woke back up, all the systems at the pitt have become
re-activated. They're functional again."

Kane paused, hand hovering over his call button.

"Didn't he just destroy your little playground? Isn't that what I just read in the notes?" Kane clarified, while
mentally double checking what this man had just said.

"No that was a different site. Compartimentalization. No, but since he woke up we've seen a rise in output at the pitt,
and more of the artifacts are coming back online. We've been so busy trying to categorize it all, that I hadn't gotten
around to telling you. Every day more technology comes back online, and we're just barely able to manage."

Kane did his level best to regain his composure. This man had dropped a bomb on his desk, not once but twice.
