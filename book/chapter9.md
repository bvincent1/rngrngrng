> UNII Corps log date `unknown`

"L99, there's a cargo hauler coming down the trail. Acknowledge." He whispered into his mike.

"Tick. Tick."

The acknowledgements were always twice. Negatives were once. Two was good, one was bad. That was the rule. O'Delt never
really thought about why it was, that was just the way it had always been. He was laying in a divot beside the road,
doing his best to blend in with the scrub and garbage that gathered around him. The smell was terrible, but he stomached
it. He had stomached a lot worse for his team, so this was nothing. He was facing the wrong way to see it coming, but he
could hear it. He did not move his head. He just waited. After a few seconds, he felt, rather saw the cargo hauler
rumble past. Big tires crunching the gravel and pebbles, electric transmission whining, and something inside banging
rythmemically away. It passed a few feet from his head, the driver completely unaware. O'Delt could not see them, but he
did not need to. They were not the target. They were just some driver making a delivery, or a pickup, or whatever. He
waited and watched as the big lumbering hauler, slowly passed him by. He could see mud and dirt splattered across the
side, deep scratches up and down the metal doors and siding, and bumpers bent out of then back into shape. The tires
were almost bald on the edges, and he thought it might slip on the corner and slide into the ditch. _More beat-up than
Etaski._ He chuckled to himself at that one.

O'Delt watched as the hauled followed the winding road, and he wondered why it had chosen now to make this trip. They
had staked out this stretch of track for three days, and nothing had disturbed them the whole time. They were on the
very edge of some corporate town with nothing but desert at their back. Flat trails on brown-grey dust wound around
various heaps of junk and in some cases actual shit. This place was a dumping ground. Nobody would bother coming here,
which was just the lost kind of style. He watched as the tires angled for the corner and acknowledge the driver's
decision to take the corner tight and cut off any traffic coming the other way. Slowly but surely the bent metal box
wound it's way back and forth, following the trail as best it could, until it faded away, past the mounds of refuse and
piles of lost.

"L54. Hauler's outside the killbox. Acknowledge." A man's voice crackled over the comms.

"Tick. Tick."

O'Delt let out a sign he did not notice he had been holding. All this work just so they could keep going. He sometimes
felt like staying at the places they visited. This was probably the 6th town he had seen in the last few months. It was
hardly different from the other. In the distance O'Delt could see the lights blinking at random on the tall towers. He
watched as the smoke obscured the sun that was just starting to dip bellow the tall buildings. Before long the city's
lights would blink on, and the night shift would take over. He thought he had heard this place was named Amotyville, but
after a while all the names blended together.
