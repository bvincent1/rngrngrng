> UNII Corps log date `unknown`

"L ninety-nine, there's a cargo hauler coming down the trail. Acknowledge." He whispered into his mike.

"Tick. Tick."

The acknowledgements were always twice. Negatives were once. Two was good, one was bad. That was the rule. O'Delt never
really thought about why it was, that was just the way it had always been. He was laying in a divot beside the road,
doing his best to blend in with the scrub and garbage that gathered around him. The smell was terrible, but he stomached
it. He had stomached a lot worse for his team, so this was nothing. He was facing the wrong way to see it coming, but he
could hear it. He did not move his head. He just waited. After a few seconds, he felt, rather saw the cargo hauler
rumble past. Six large worn tires crunching on gravel and pebbles, whining electric transmission, and something inside
banging rhythmically away. It passed just a few feet from his head, the driver completely unaware he was even there.
O'Delt could not see them, but he did not need to. They were not the target. They were just some nobody making a
delivery, or a pickup, or whatever. He waited and watched as the big lumbering hauler, slowly passed him by. He could
see mud and dirt splattered across the side, deep scratches up and down the metal doors and siding, and bumpers bent out
of - then back into shape. The tires were almost bald on the edges, and he thought it might slip on a corner and slide
into the ditch. _More beat-up than Etaski._ He chuckled to himself at that one.

O'Delt watched as the hauler followed the winding road, and he wondered why it had chosen now to make this trip. They
had staked out this stretch of track for seven days, and nothing had disturbed them the whole time. They were on the
very edge of some corporate town with nothing but desert to their back. Flat trails on brown-grey dust wound around
various heaps of junk and in some cases actual shit. This place was a dumping ground. Nobody would bother coming here,
which was just the Lost kind of style. He watched as the tires angled for the corner and acknowledge the driver's
decision to take the corner tight and cut off any traffic coming the other way. Slowly but surely the bent metal box
wound it's way back and forth, following the trail as best it could, until it faded away, past the mounds of refuse and
piles of waste past where he could watch, but still within their operations site. Several minutes passed.

"L fifty four. Hauler's outside the killbox. Acknowledge." A man's voice crackled over the comms.

"Tick. Tick."

O'Delt let out a sign he did not notice he had been holding. All this work just so they could keep going. This was
probably the six'th town he had seen in the last few months, but it was hardly different from the others. Slowly O'Delt
turned his head until he could see the lights blinking on towers in the far off distance. He watched as the smoke and
soot from warehouse towers and manufactory stacks rose steadily to choke out the sunlight. The whole city was littered
with stacks, each one working to power or refine some resource into something else. He had heard that this city was a
manufacturing center, working to refine and rework old scrap into something usable again. It was at great cost. The
surrounding landscape had been terraformed into a barren waste. The soot drifter out, and eventually settled around the
city, strangling it, and forcing everyone deeper inside it. Then the cycle began again. Materials burnt, resources
refined, waste tossed, and people shrinking away from their mistakes. Maybe that was why they kept moving. They were
running from their mistakes. _Certainly a lot of those._ O'Delt sighed consciously this time. The waits between always
brought out the morose memories. He did not want to remember how his life had been. He tried to push himself, to throw
himself into this life, and to exceed his fellow teammates. They probably did the same, and together that was what made
them so good at their jobs. He could not imagine what would compel someone to stay in a place like this. To stir and
wallow in the same mistakes, the same problems, and same cycle. That would have driven him crazy. All they had to do was
leave. Surely there was nothing worth a damn that was keeping them here. Surely they could see that they would be
trapped.

"L forty two. Concrete valor." A another voice crackled, fainter this time, over the com net.

The infiltration team had found their target and were calling it in. O'Delt felt a small rush, and shifted just
slightly. He tapped his shortened assault rifle, tucked firmly next to his chest and protruding under his arm. He did
his best to check the action without disturbing the trash and dust that enshrouded him. With his hood up, and his jacket
loose, he blended in perfectly. Nobody would be able to find him. Except another Lost. Maybe. He tucked his head into
his chest and worked to make the outline visible of his rifle. He slowly inched his free hand and felt the cold metal
stock, then the carrier, and then the breach. He pulled on the charging handle slightly and confirmed it was loaded. He
carefully released and let out the breath he had been again holding. The last thing he wanted to do was foul the action.
Satisfied with his check, he slowly untucked his head and resumed watch. He knew he would not see anything, and even
that was probably the point. The infiltration team was rendezvousing with the north side of their formation. He was just
a safety measure. It always chapped him that he was sidelined. This was the third time he had been assigned outwatch,
but he had not made a complaint of it. He would never complain. O'Delt dreamt of someday having his own command, and
leading an infiltration team. Together him and his team would lead a flawless grab, and score big time. He would prove
his worth. He would earn the respect of the rest of them. But today was not it. O'Delt scanned the trail. Wondering what
the others were getting into. He knew who was on the mission. Some of their best were their. Zetes, Takolov, Lechii, and
even the legendary DeKap. O'Rhonn was on the team too. God he hated that guy. O'Rhonn was just an asshole in general,
but he had always had it out for him. He would probably never hear the end of it. Who knows, maybe he would get lucky
and catch a round in the neck. O'Rhonn silenced forever. O'Delt felt bad about that, he knew their medic was on her last
legs. They all worried how much longer Philo would hang on, and nobody wanted to deal with her replacement. Ep. At least
O'Delt was not Epi. That guy had it rough.

"Break! I said break!" a voice yelled through the comms.

O'Delt's blood ran cold. It took every once of his training to not reflexively twist and crane his head right, try to
see what the emergency was. He knew it was bad. He slowly, excruciatingly turned his head, and craned his eyes in their
sockets towards the opposite flank. He wanted to see if there was smoke, explosions, or maybe stray fire. Anything to
tell him what was going on. He strained all his senses, trying to absorb anything to tell him what was happening.

"We've been made! There's corpo forces! We're bugging out!" The same voice yelled.

In the background O'Delt thought he heard yelling. He couldn't identify who had called it, but clearly they were about
to get hit. _Action._ He waited for orders to come through. He was on the main band, which was where they would get new
directives. He waited. Waited. It felt like precious seconds where going by, and nobody was responding. He had half a
mind to try hailing his team lead, but he knew better than to break protocol. Some recent wins had them feeling
confident. Maybe it had finally caught up with them.

"This is one, everyone stay where you are. L thirty two, I need a status report now." O'Delt felt a surge of relief.
This was who he had wanted to hear. Alphason. Their commander. Their leader. Some even said their best. He had been the
one who had banded them all together and turned them from lost, into the Lost.

"Thirty-two! We've been ambushed! Twenty-one sent up the flare, and we only just got out!"

O'Delt recognized Zetes' voice over to comms.

"Sixty-two and fifty-one booth took rounds! We're in transit!"

That was bad. Takolov and Lechii were down.

"We lost twenty-one." Zetes' voice lowered. "MIA."

O'Delt felt that loss in his gut. In his head, he rationalized that DeKap was just missing. That he would show up
somewhere later. He was a legendary scout. He had come back from impossible odds before. O'Delt pushed his worries deep
down. He tried to focus on himself. Centered.

"Over." Zetes' voice crackled over the comms.

"One acknowledges. Meet up at rally point two. Exactly relentless. Acknowledge." Alphason's steely voice crackled over
the comms.

"Tick. Tick."

"Tick. Tick."

"Tick. Tick."

That was the signal. Alphason was going to send re-enforcements. He was not going to abandon the team. The teams had
sent their acknowledgements, and now they were all getting ready for the next order. O'Delt did a silent thanks that he
had already done a weapons check. He also cursed himself that perhaps he had left his gear unstowed back at camp. He
could not remember if he had thought to pack it up like he was supposed to or not. Too late now. He Thought about
risking it, sending a message back, but he would get chewed for sure if he broke comms. It was not dire yet. Maybe they
would had time.

"Fifth squad, and second squad, rally two. The rest rally 1, palace universe." Alphason's voice rang clear again.

That was it. They had their orders. His squad had been ordered to support the infiltration team, and the others had been
order to pack up. They were going to hit hard, and then leave fast. His squad, fifth had been on the far edge of the
site, next to third squad. They were not the closest but they would be the best positioned to fold into the corpos and
chew them up from another angle.

"fifth squad! sepulcher casting!"

A new voice yelled out over the global channel. That was his command, DeLam. O'Delt had just been ordered to switch to
their squad comms. O'Delt was halfway to reaching his belt where he kept his comm pack.

"Second squad! sepulcher casting!"

O'Delt Recognized the second squad commands voice, Betchek. He finally reached his comms box, and mentally counted the
clicks to get to his squad channel.

"-ove forwards into the city, and get a cross fire. Rally 2 is the choke, get there, get into position." He tunned in.
Lam was clearly reved up. This was a new command for him, since their squad command Lechii had been selected for the
infiltration. O'Delt did not mind Lechii. A good man, if a bit loose on regulations and rules.

"We're supporting first, lets make it look good! Any questions?" He hastily added "Open comms."

"How far is rally 2?" O'Delt asked, knowing full well they would be cutting it close.

"Uhhh, it's-" Lam faded out, clearly searching for the location on his tablet. "It'ssss west four, two north. And that's
the route we're gonna take."

"Let's get moving then." a gruff female voice, Epsis, grunted over the comms.

"Fifth squad, move out!" Lam yelled.

O'Delt felt his heart skip a beat. Now was his chance to prove himself. Show the others that he was worth it. He pushed
off the ground, and stood up to his full height. He was the at the edge of their formation, positioned closest to the
town. He glanced around, and he saw figures take off from 2 different foxholes that had been completely hidden from the
road. He saw them take off at a quick pace, and he quickly matched it. He almost angled straight towards them, but then
he decided to keep to the flank. He would keep his role of scout to heart. O'Delt knew that it was only a brevet role.
Normally a prestigious role in a squad, but today it was an excuse to keep him out of danger. Still he was determined.

He pulled his bullpup configured riffle close, and took off towards the derelict buildings at the edge of the city.
Horribly rusted metal, and cracked waist high brick broke up the limits, and he effortlessly leapt over them. One
obstacle after the other, he charged forwards. Out of the corner of his eye he saw fifth squad off to his side, matching
his pace, but angling for a more direct route in line with a gravel road.
