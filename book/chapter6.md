> UNII Corps log date 2327-09-30-0505 20m later

The world exploded into tiny little fragments.

It did that periodically, but on a random schedule. Hector couldn't predict it, but he always felt a bit less present
when it reassembled itself around him. He watched as the pieces arranged themselves into tiny little shapes, and then
the shapes slowly formed into recognizable things. People, things, places. They all reversed their fractal expansions,
returning perfectly into place. If he squinted, he could almost see the pieces before they actually materialized. But
that took a lot of effort, and Hector was exhausted. He felt thin and frail, but he knew he wasn't. It was this dream,
this endless dream that had him running across town, chasing things and people. And now he was in a room with the people
he did not know, and they were wasting time talking about things that did not matter. He had to make sure they did what
he needed. So that the dream could stop.

_What did I need again? I thought it was clear, but now I can't quite put my finger on it anymore._

Involuntarily, he winced. His brain was bringing up bad memories, bad feelings. Inwardly he shuddered at the
recollection, but outwardly he was fixed in place. He started this dream with some terrible feeling, but the feeling was
gone now, and so he could think again.

_All I need is to do is find it. Find the fix._

Hector didn't have to look around to feel these strangers arguing and talking amongst each other. He found he didn't
have to look at things anymore to feel them, but when he did look at them, he felt he could see everything about them in
one glance. It was like looking through a kaleidoscope, but each reflection was moving independently of the other. He
could see everything that ever happened, and lots of the stuff that could happen, all at once. It was exhausting him
each time he did it. He'd seen so much death, and soo much pain in too many people. With infinite time and space came
infinite suffering. Not just the suffering of others, but his own suffering as well. He saw all his own mistakes, and
mistakes that he hadn't even made but thought of, played out in an endless torrent of thought. It was all there for him
to suffer through.

"..wants us to find out" Hector spoke without realizing it.

The others turned to look at him, and Hector glimpsed their fractal halos that only he could see. The boy, now a man,
with his idyllic view of the past, the woman forcing her story to align to her need for success, and the other woman
searching for meaning in a world without one. He envied them all. His past was mired in failure, and he was suffocating
under it's weight.

"We should bring him along," the man said. O'Delt to his friends.

Far away, Hector watched as Arthur O'Delt was eaten alive by the monsters waiting for him. He screamed, and fought, but
they pulled him under. Arthur O'Delt was gone. Hector knew this path has fraught with danger, but he couldn't tell them
how dangerous it was. If they knew they were sure to fail. He'd seen that too. He needed them alive, there was a path
through the horrors that awaited them. A a narrow gap between the towering horrors they were destined to face. That was
how they'd win, not by meeting it head on, but by skirting around the edge. He had to keep them on the path, that very
narrow path.

He felt a pull. A pull from far away, tugging ever so slightly at his soul. He ignored it, trying to coral his
directionless, errant thoughts into something workable. His mind was constantly floating. He could feel different
thoughts intrude into his psyche. A constant, slow, pulsing against his mind. Mostly the thoughts were his own, but also
not. They always seemed to be reacting differently. They existed differently. Parallel to him now, but along different
paths and vectors along some higher dimensionality. Sometimes they pushed into his consciousness and he felt his body,
usually his mouth, acting out of turn.

But there was an even bigger presence. He'd felt it at the blok. He'd tapped into it. The presence was a font of barely
restrained power. The surge had almost torn his consciousness from his body, but somehow he'd held on. Now he felt it
again. It was swimming nearby, some great and ancient force hovering just outside his reach. Hector could feel the
ripples it left in it's wake. From this distance, he bobbed only a little on the waves, but as he got closer, it'd get
worse. Hector dreaded having to approach the source of his discomfort, but he needed it. Old habits die hard and he
needed the release. It tugged at him, a familiar urge. A muted pull like in his old days, but still their.

Hector returned to the room, refocusing his thoughts to the present and current space. They were arguing about
something. Hector wanted to warn them, tell them that this was all pointless and that they should abandon this search
and join him on his quest, but that would not work. He needed to push them, to nudge them ever so slightly without their
knowledge so that they thought it was their idea, not his. That had always worked int he past, but it was tough then,
and even tougher now. Hector struggled to remember, struggled to focus on the here and now. it was like being in a
storm. It was tempting to only be conscious of the near, but if you pushed, if you pushed yourself you found you could
see past the haze and the torrent. Hector found the storm disorienting, but he knew the anser was out there. All he
needed to do was to get close enough. He would see it, without loosing himself to the noise that surrounded them at all
times.

"How far is it." He heard Andrea Ryan say. She was annoyed, but he could see past her facade into the scars that haunted
her soul.

More arguing followed and Hector felt himself walk towards one of the monitors. He thought he saw something there. A
shadow. A gap in the storm that should not be there. He could almost pick it out, see a shape, when it snapped shut and
the torrent resumed.

"I'll book it, quit your bitching." The Rikki Mega said, walking back towards the desk hector was standing in front of.
"Excuse me please." she said, and Hector stepped out of her way. He saw she was scared and worried, but he could also
see she confused by what she was saying, something was bothering her. He ignored it, and put all his effort info
focusing on the current path.

He watched as Andrea Ryan punched a hole in the wall, narrowly missing Del. Del returned by drawing his holstered pistol
and shoving it into her chest. The two paused a fraction of a second, before Del pulled the trigger and a hollow thump
echoed through the room.

_No, we gotta focus._ Hector willed his mind to the present, and all was as he left it. They were all stepping into a
car and he was still on the way towards their destination. Hector did not use to believe in a god, but now he prayed
that these two would forgive him afterwards. Forgive him for what they would face, and for what he would put them
through. Hector felt himself drift through time, carried like a feather on the breeze by his prayers. He was at peace,
forfeiting his willpower to something bigger than himself. He tried to stay like that, suspended in time and space, but
eventually he felt an unease that made him open his eyes. The storm had subsided, and he felt a great absence wash over
him. A chill crept down his spine as he felt a deep presence nearby, and at once he knew that the prayer had brought it
close. It was the same as at the blok, but this time his connection was closer to it. He could eak an intension from the
entity. Within that emptiness, under the shadow from the storm, Hector could feel it. Hunger. A great and unending
hunger. Entropy embodied. The descent into oblivion. He could barely hold on to his mind, and he felt the power pulsing
through him like a conduit. Hector screamed, and tensed, tried to break free. And did.

His mind reconnected to his body. He was in the back seat of a car, calmly sitting next to his crew. They did not react,
or seem unnerved by him. He was alone in his suffering.

"Pull up at the corner there" Arthur O'Delt said to the driver.
